The First Dinosaur Skeleton

In 1824, English geologist William Buckland scientifically described Megalosaurus, the first dinosaur to be formally named, based on fossils discovered in Oxfordshire, England. This discovery began humanity's fascination with prehistoric life.

The word Dinosauria was coined in 1842 by Sir Richard Owen. Since then, thousands of fossils have been uncovered across every continent, transforming our understanding of evolution and extinction.
